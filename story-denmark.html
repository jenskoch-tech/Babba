School Years · 1960s

A Trip across the Border

Danish Farm Life

✦

In the late 1950’s - early 60’s - the German “Wirtschaftswunder” (“Economic Miracle”), also known as the Miracle on the Rhine, as the rapid reconstruction and development of the economies of West Germany and Austria after World War II.) was at its peek. Suddenly people could afford cars, TVs and were able to travel beyond the borders of Germany. The most favorable destination was Italy and it turned out to be every year more and more like a Teutonic invasion.

Others - like us - were not so fortunate and had to spend the summer vacation either at home in Frankfurt or alternatively our desperate parents were sending us away to our grandparents up North into a small village in the Harz mountains. That place had 500 souls as inhabitants, two grocery stores, two bakeries, one dairy factory, one church but more importantly two pubs which contributed immensely to the joyful entertainment of the adult male population. But also that lead to the occasional disapproval of the female population. However, even though we had a good time at the village during the summer it was always annoying when we got back to school and then had to write an essay about your most exciting or adventurous event during the summer vacation. What could you write? It was not there, not much came to my mind. The one summer my friend and I were surprised by my friend’s parents offering us to stay the summer in Denmark. Wow, that was exciting!

The destination was not Italy but it was somehow exotic, another country which you could later maybe brag about. Then after a short overnight stop in Hamburg we arrived in Denmark at a little village with a rondel decorated by blooming flowers, the white painted houses showed benches in front of them where old ladies were sitting smoking cigars (!). That was encouraging as we pictured it must be fun here as people seemed to be so liberal. We arrived at our final destination which turned out to be a farm. Mmh!

Is there a difference between a Danish farm and a German farm? No! Not really!

Our hosts, the Danish farmer’s family were friendly and as a warm welcome we were offered a opulent lunch. The next day our vacation really started. The farmer showed us around and then instructed us to our surprise what chorus we had to do. It started with weeding out some spots around the farm with a scythe, a tool we had seen before but never used. Therefore it was a clumsy job. The next task was to clear out weeds from and endless field of beetroots. The following weeks were filled with a variety of challenging jobs: cleaning the pig stall and the cow and horse stalls, then making hay with horse and/or tractor, melking cows with a melking machine, etc.

One day we were instructed to come to the pig stall (again?) where the sow had a whole bunch of new born piglings. Then the farmer gave some pliers and asked us to break off all teeth of the little swines. We almost laughed, as we thought it was a joke. But no, that was serious business as we learned the reason for that. It was due to the fact that the little piglings with their teeth wood hurt the sow and therefore needed to be removed and the piglings would otherwise not survive. So we advanced to a semi professional animal “dentists”.

Yes, we got stronger everyday as we worked hard and were fed five times a day but our expectations to have some different kind of fun were diminished as we were very tired at the end of every day.

We only made it a few times to the beach, we were invited once to a Danish restaurant with an impressive five tier platter filled with all kind of delicious seafood which we very much enjoyed. We also visited the Sonderburg Castle as a cultural highlight.
Before the trip we had fantasized to meet some blonde Danish girls but that illusion faded away like a fata morgana. The only female species we encountered were the farmer’s wife and the maid. The girls were not to be seen, they probably were on vacation - in Italy? -

After this vacation with all the “exciting” experience we were very careful and suspicious towards parents or other well meaning adults when it came to any offers to visit any foreign country without giving in advance specific details about the locations and the particular circumstances.
