The Aquarium

Story from Peter's early Military Days

✦

In 1972 I was assigned to a new post at a German Air Force depot for high tech aircraft spare parts as the First Sergeant. That included the promotion to Sergeant which made me at that time the youngest First Sergeant of the Air Force. The depot was hideously located in the Forest near Neustadt an der Weinstrasse and consisted of pre -World War II old buildings and warehouses which were previously used as a factory of some kind.


After a while I had adopted to the new environment and proudly accepted the challenges of the daily duties.
One day our Captain the commanding officer called me and another Sergeant into his fairly large office that was probably at a time used by the Director of the factory.

The Captain announced that he was going to go on a vacation for two weeks pretty soon and gave us orders how to deal with the daily duties during his absence. Then he pointed at his somewhat huge aquarium which was located on the wall on the right of the door to his office. He asked us to take very good care of the aquarium during his vacation. This task included the general maintenance of the aquarium, the control of the water temperature and the cleanness of the filter and water pump. But more importantly the feeding of the exotic fishes - in particular the black mollies as the Captain’s favorite species of the aquarium - was his main concern.

Hence the sergeant was appointed as the guardian of the aquarium and its vulnerable inhabitants while my task was to remind the Sergeant of that special duty on a daily basis and to supervise his performance.

Even though we felt that we were under less pressure from the Captain while he was away the given task was kind of a burden. However, everything went fine for a while.

But one day we stared at the aquarium and found two black mollies swimming keel up in the water. What a shock! We panicked! What to do? We immediately pictured the potential upcoming punishment from the Captain and were desperate.

Then we finally came up with the glorious idea to drive in a hurry to the pet store. There we showed the salesman the two deceased black mollies asking him to urgently find us replacements which needed to look as similar as possible as those we lost due to unknown circumstances.

Back at depot we placed the “reborn” fishes into the aquarium praying that they would adopt the new environment and prosper quickly. Our instant hope was the Captain would not notice any variation in the aquatic population. Upon the Captain’s return from his vacation we were eager to find out if he would actually notice any difference.

For a few days there was no negative reaction by the Captain. But on another day he by shaking his head expressed some suspicions mentioning that something might not be right. Suddenly our fear for consequent punishment rose substantially. Consequently we inspired ourselves to come up with a brilliant idea to derail the Captain’s exponential focus on the aquarium and his so admired fauna within.

Here was the idea. We went down to our mess hall’s kitchen and asked the kitchen lady to hand us over two chicken out of the freezer. Naturally she asked for the purpose of this request but also offered to cook the chicken if needed. We thanked her gratefully but pointed out that we had to prepare something very special for our Captain.

Then the Sergeant as the designated guardian of the aquarium and its precious contents with the help of another Sergeant went upstairs one story above the Captain’s office. They attached each of the two chicken on a stick with a string and then lowered the two naked birds down to each of the two large windows of the Captain’s office and moved those creatures back and forth simulating flying movements.

At the same time I had entered the Captain’s office pretending I had some issues to discuss with him. While our conversation was in process I acted as I was distracted by something I saw at the windows.

Finally the Captain noticed that and also looked at the windows. His reaction was: “What in the world is that???” , “ Do I see naked chicken flying at my windows or what?”

I immediately confirmed the mysterious happening and waited until he started laughing and I laughed as well. The Captain then asked for the reason of this alienated performance. We finally confessed that we wanted to create some distraction for him so that his attention would not be so heavily focused on the aquarium and his exotic fishes and rather more drawn to other species of the animal world.

The Captain showed mercy and forgiveness and we escaped any severe punishment. At the end we were released from that chore. Somebody else more trustworthy and with a more serious attitude was appointed for that duty taking care of the well being of the finny tribe in the event of any future absences of the Captain.
